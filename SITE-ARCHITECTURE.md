## PAGE STRUCTURE — 7-SECTION PATTERN

Every program page and sub-service page follows this EXACT structure. Do not deviate.

### Section 1: Hero (Dark Gradient Background)
- H1 heading with primary keyword
- 2-3 line description with location signals
- CTA button ("Enroll Now" or "Book Free Consultation")
- Trust badges (certified NLP, Harvard-affiliated, MSME registered)

### Section 2: Why It Matters
- Problem-agitation section
- Why this skill/service is critical in today's world
- Statistics, relatable scenarios, emotional hooks
- Location context (Delhi, NCR, Indian professional landscape)

### Section 3: What You'll Develop / What's Covered (6-8 Cards)
- Grid of skill cards (icon + title + short description)
- Each card = one skill/outcome the student will gain
- Use specific, tangible outcomes, not vague promises

### Section 4: Who It's For (5-6 Cards with Internal Cross-Links)
- Audience cards showing different personas
- EACH CARD LINKS to a relevant program or service page (internal linking)
- Example: "Working Professionals" card → links to /for/professionals
- Example: "Women seeking confidence" → links to /programs/self-mastery-women

### Section 5: Why We're Different (4-6 Differentiators)
- Unique selling points of Hidden Potential Skills
- E-E-A-T signals: Supreet's credentials, certifications, experience
- Comparison with generic training (without naming competitors)
- Harvard affiliation, NLP certification, graphology expertise

### Section 6: FAQ (6 Questions with FAQPage Schema)
- 6 keyword-optimised questions and answers
- Covers pricing, duration, mode, location, outcomes, eligibility
- MUST have FAQPage schema markup
- Questions should target long-tail keywords people actually search

### Section 7: CTA Section (Dark Background)
- Final call-to-action
- Cross-sell related programs/services (2-3 internal links)
- "Enroll Now" or "Book Free Consultation" button
- WhatsApp contact option

## SEO SPECIFICATIONS (Apply to EVERY page)

### Content Requirements
- **Word count:** 2,000-2,500+ words per program/service page
- **Primary keyword:** 10+ natural mentions throughout the page
- **LSI keywords:** Semantic variations woven throughout body copy
- **Location signals:** 15+ per page (Delhi, Dwarka, Sector 19B, NCR, South West Delhi, New Delhi, Noida, Gurgaon, Faridabad)
- **E-E-A-T signals:** Supreet's credentials mentioned in every page (NLP cert, Harvard life skills, graphology, years of experience)

### Schema Markup (per page)
- **Service schema** on all service pages
- **Course schema** on all program pages
- **FAQPage schema** on every page with FAQ section
- **Organization schema** on homepage
- **LocalBusiness schema** on location pages
- **BreadcrumbList schema** on all pages

### Internal Linking Strategy
- Every page links to 7-9 other pages on the site
- Sub-service pages link to parent programs
- Programs link to related sub-services
- All pages link to Enroll and Contact
- "Who It's For" section = primary internal linking vehicle
- Location pages link to all services available in that area

### Meta Tags
- Unique meta title per page (under 60 chars, keyword + location + brand)
- Unique meta description (under 155 chars, keyword + CTA + differentiator)
- OG image, OG title, OG description for social sharing
- Twitter card meta tags

## KEY NOTES FOR BUILDERS

1. NEVER use generic AI-sounding copy. Every sentence should feel like Supreet wrote it.
2. Location signals are NOT just "in Delhi" at the end of sentences — weave them naturally (mention Dwarka, Sector 19B, South West Delhi, NCR, specific areas).
3. E-E-A-T is not a section — it's embedded THROUGHOUT the copy. Mention certifications, experience, student count in the flow of content.
4. The "Who It's For" section is the PRIMARY internal linking mechanism — every card should link to another page.
5. FAQ questions should target long-tail keywords people actually Google — not generic "What is NLP?" type questions.
6. Assessment page is LAST — waiting on client input for evaluation methodology. Skip it until told otherwise.
